Question - Changes

5. How would this dataset change the way you think about space?

6. How does this dataset change across sydney?

7. How does it relate to the latte line?

8. How has the dataset changed between 2011 and 2016?




Answer

5. Space not only refers to ‘area’ but also refers to dynamically changing living data. This is affected by various reasons involving societal, ethical, educational level and and more.

6. The change of dataset across Sydney is displayed around North-west and West areas where the majority of the population is accommodated by younger generations (34 years and under). Dataset shows that people tend to favour living near transportation and areas with recent major residential developments.

7. There is a distinct difference of the areas that are placed above and below the latte line. The area above the latte line is known to be a 'traditionally rich area' (North and North east shore). This is due to older generation being the main occupants of the area. In contrary, the area below the latte line is seen to be largely occupied by younger generations (34 years and under). This is mostly due to the area being more affordable for the younger generations.

8. In 2011, people were mostly populated in major cities, especially Western side of Sydney. However, between 2011 and 2016, due to the residential boom and government support in building additional transportation has resulted in distribution of the dense population from areas such as Blacktown, Parramatta and Kellyville to satellite suburbs such as the Ponds, Westmead and more. In 2016, increasing statistics (ABS census) show that in areas such as Zetland, Rhodes and surrounding areas has grown in population due to the huge residential (apartments) developments.
